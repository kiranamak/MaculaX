About the Technology

Our Technology

We employ an innovative use of nanopores to determine the structure and sequence of proteins.

Learn more about how the technology works

Nanopores

Nanopores can sequence DNA and identify all 20 amino acids. As the molecule is passed through the nano-scale hole, it blocks the flow of ionic current. The changing current can be used to determine the amino acid composition.

ATP Synthase

Traditional nanopore sequencing has only examined one amino acid at a time, but a will not appear as a single neat chain. To be able to gain enough information to decompose the current data to determine 3d structure, we plan to rotate the protein using ATP Synthase.

Analysis

Given the electrical current cross section data obtained from multiple angles, we we will synthesize it to break down the 3d protein sequence and structure.

Nanopores

Nanopores can measure the blockage of ionic current.

A nanopore is a nano-scale hole within a membrane. Ionic current is repeatedly passed through the pore. When imaging a molecule, the molecule gets passed through the pore and limits the flow of current. Based on the resulting readout, we can determine the composition. The diagram to the right demonstrates how this can be used to sequence DNA.

A protein will work similarly, however, there are some crucial differences. Proteins are made of 20 different amino acids instead of the four base pairs that make up DNA. Further, proteins are folded and will not be fed through one amino acid at a time. Therefore, it will take multiple passes to be able to determine the composition of the protein. How this works will be detailed in the next section.

ATP Synthase for Rotation

To get a full sequential and structural understanding of the protein we need to collect more than one data point. Each time the protein goes through the nanopore we get layers of cross-sections each with their own current reading. By rotating the protein through mutliple angles we can get various angles of cross-sections.


This overlapping data will provide enough information to determine the 3d structure of the protein.

Analysis

Analysis and imaging of protein structure and sequence

Using data from the nanopore, we can reconstruct a model of the protein with accurate sequence and structural information. Software will allow for visualization techniques.


We also use machine learning to more easily be able to detect structural motifs such as alpha helices and beta pleated sheets.
